After 5 years of building software to help social workers support children at <a href="https://kfh.org/" target="_blank">Koinonia Family Services</a>, I am ready for a new adventure! It's been wonderful growing together with the team there through leadership changes, shifts in the field, and evolving technology. I am super grateful to have worked on back-end, database systems, and AI engineering after years of focusing more on front-end mobile web, accessibility, and teaching. Maintaining a big legacy system with hundreds of thousands of lines of code to stay compliant with ever changing regulations is an enourmous responsibility! I'll be gradually transitioning over the next 3-4 months and then taking time over the holidays to recharge before the new year.

So, what's next? I'm not exactly sure, and that is terrifying! While on one hand it is a privilige and I am  fortunate to be able to take a break from work, the fear around money is real and pausing cuts direclty against our conditioning as consumers in this capitalist world. I do believe it is important and even necessary to face those feelings head on so we can heal our materialist culture. Check out my other posts on <a href="/meditation">meditation</a> and <a href="/food-forests-and-natural-building">food forests</a> if you are curious.

I'm hoping to find a way to combine tech skills and a meditation practice with a calling to help serve mother earth and people who live here, although I'm not exactly sure what that will look like!
